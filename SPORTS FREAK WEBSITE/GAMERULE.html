MASTER THE SPIRIT OF CRICKET🏏

Cricket is a bat-and-ball sport where two teams compete to score the most runs.

🎯Objective

  • Score more runs than the opposing team.

  • The batting team tries to score as many runs as possible.

  • The bowling team tries to stop runs and take wickets.

  • After both teams bat, the team with the higher score wins.

👥 Players

  • 2 teams

  • 11 players in each team.

🪙 Toss

  • Captains toss a coin.

  • winner chooses to bat or bowl first.

🏏 Batting

  • Two batters play at a time.

  • Score runs by running or hitting boundaries.

⚾ Bowling

  • Bowler delivers the ball to the batter.

  • 1 over = 6 legal balls.

🛡️Fielding

  • Fielders stop the ball.

  • They catch the ball or run out batters.

🏃 Runs

  • Running between wickets = 1 or more runs.

  • Ball reaches boundary = 4 runs.

  • Ball clears boundary = 6 runs.

🧑‍⚖️Officials

  1. Two umpires

  2. hird umpire

  3. Match referee

📤 Ways to Get Out

  1. Bowled - Ball hits the stumps.

  2. Caught - Fielder catches the ball before it touches the ground.

  3. LBW - Ball hits the batter's leg in front of the stumps.

  4. Run Out - Bails are removed before the batter reaches the crease.

  5. Stumped - Wicketkeeper removes the bails when the batter is outside the crease.

  6. Hit Wicket - Batter accidentally hits their own stumps.

🔄 Innings

  • One team's turn to bat.

  • Team 1 bats first.

  • Team 2 chases the target.

🎯 Target

  • One more run than the opponent's score.

🏆 Winner

  • Team with more runs wins.

📜 Key Terms

  • Wicket - A batter getting out.

  • Over - 6 legal balls.

  • Crease - Safe line for batters.

  • Boundary - Rope marking the edge of the field.

  • No Ball - Illegal delivery; batting team gets 1 extra run.

  • Wide Ball - Ball too far to hit, batting team gets 1 extra run.

  • Bye - Runs scored when the ball misses the bat and body.

  • Leg Bye - Runs scored when the ball hits the batter's body.

  • Maiden Over - An over with no runs scored.

  • Duck - Batter gets out for 0 runs.

  • Century -Batter scores 100 runs.

  • Half - Century-Batter scores 50 runs.

  • Hat - Trick-Bowler takes 3 wickets in 3 consecutive balls.

  • Powerplay - Fielding restrictions in the first overs.

  • DRS - Video review system for umpire decisions.

BASIC RULES AND KEY TERMS OF FOOTBALL⚽

Football is a team sport where two teams try to score goals by kicking the ball into the opponent’s net.

🚀 Objective

  • Score more goals than the opposing team.

👥 Players

  • 11 players in each team, including one goalkeeper.

🪙 Kick-Off

  • The match starts with a kick-off from the center circle.

⏱️ Match Duration

  • 90 minutes, divided into two 45-minute halves.

⚽ Ball Movement

  • Players pass, dribble, and shoot using their feet.

🧤 Goalkeeper

  • The goalkeeper can use their hands inside the penalty area.

🚀 Goal

  • A goal is scored when the ball fully crosses the goal line.

🚫 Offside

  • An attacking player must not be ahead of the last defender when receiving the ball.

Fouls

  • Illegal actions such as pushing, tripping, or handball.

🟨 Yellow Card

  • Warning for unsporting behavior.

🟥 Red Card

  • Player is sent off and cannot continue.

🔁 Substitutions

  • Teams can replace players during the match.

👨‍⚖️ Officials

  • Referee and assistant referees enforce the rules.

🏆 Winner

  • The team with more goals wins.

📜 Key Terms

  • Goal - One point scored.

  • Corner Kick - Awarded when the ball crosses the goal line off a defender.

  • Free Kick - Awarded after a foul.

  • Penalty Kick - Shot taken from the penalty spot after a foul inside the penalty area.

  • Throw-In - Used to restart play when the ball goes out on the sideline.

  • Offside - Attacker is in an illegal position.

RISE AS A CHAMPION🥊

Boxing is a battle of mental toughness and physical strength.

⚡ Objective

  • Score more points than your opponent or win by knockout.

👥 Participants

  • 2 boxers compete in the ring.

  • Each boxer fights in a specific weight class.

⏳Match Duration

  • Amateur boxing: 3 rounds of 3 minutes.

  • Professional boxing: 4 to 12 rounds of 3 minutes.

🧤 Essential Equipment

  • Boxing Gloves

  • Mouthguard

  • Hand Wraps

  • Boxing Shoes

  • Protective Headgear (amateur)

🥊 Legal Punches

  • Only punches with the knuckle part of a closed glove are allowed.

  • Punches must land on the front or sides of the head and torso above the belt.

🛡️ Defensive Moves

  • Blocking

  • Slipping

  • Ducking

  • Weaving

🚫 Fouls

  • Hitting below the belt.

  • Headbutting.

  • Holding excessively.

  • Hitting after the bell.

  • Punching the back of the head.

⚠️ Penalties

  • Warning from the referee.

  • Point deduction.

  • Disqualification.

🚩Knockdown Rule

  • If a boxer is knocked down, the referee counts to 10.

  • If the boxer stands up before 10 and can continue, the fight goes on.

  • If not, the opponent wins by knockout.

🏆 Ways to Win

  • Knockout (KO)

  • Technical Knockout (TKO)

  • Points Decision

  • Opponent Disqualification

🤝 Sportsmanship

  • Boxers must respect the referee, follow the rules, and compete fairly.

MASTER THE ART OF CHESS♟️

Chess is the ultimate test of sharp minds and strategic thinking.

🎯 Objective

  • Checkmate your opponent’s king by putting it under attack with no legal escape.

      • 👥 Players

      • 2 players compete against each other.

⬜ Chessboard

  • The board has 64 squares.

  • Arranged in an 8 × 8 grid.

♟️ Total Pieces

  • Each player starts with 16 pieces.

  • Total pieces on the board: 32 pieces.

Piece Breakdown Per Player

  • 👑 1 King

  • ♛ 1 Queen

  • ♜ 2 Rooks

  • ♝ 2 Bishops

  • ♞ 2 Knights

  • ♟️ 8 Pawns

▶️ How the Game Starts

  • White always moves first.

  • Players take turns moving one piece at a time.

♟️ How the Pieces Move

  • 👑 King — One square in any direction.

  • ♛ Queen — Any number of squares in any direction.

  • ♜ Rook — Horizontally or vertically.

  • ♝ Bishop — Diagonally.

  • ♞ Knight — In an L-shape.

  • ♟️ Pawn — Moves forward, captures diagonally.

✨ Special Moves

  • 🏰 Castling - A move that helps protect the king and activate the rook.

  • ♟️ En Passant - A special pawn capture under specific conditions.

  • 👑 Pawn Promotion - A pawn reaching the last rank is replaced by another piece, usually a queen.

⚠️ Important Rules

  • You cannot leave your king in check.

  • If your king is attacked, you must respond immediately.

🏁 Ways the Game Ends

  • 👑 Checkmate — Win.

  • 🤝 Stalemate — Draw.

  • 🔁 Threefold Repetition — Draw.

  • ⏳ 50-Move Rule — Draw.

  • ⏰ Time Out — Loss on time.

  • 🤝 Draw by Agreement.

🏆 How to Win

  • Deliver checkmate or win on time.

🧠 Basic Strategy Tips

  • Control the center.

  • Develop your pieces.

  • Protect your king.

  • Think ahead.
